# 17 OSINT Tools Every Hacker Must Know

### This video breaks down 17 powerful OSINT tools used by real investigators, ethical hackers, analysts, and cyber-sleuths.

> Disclaimer: These tools must be used for legal and ethical purposes only. Always obtain proper authorization before performing any investigation or analysis. ⚠️ 
>

## 1. Sherlock

* **GitHub:** [https://github.com/sherlock-project/sherlock](https://github.com/sherlock-project/sherlock)
* Searches for usernames across multiple social networks.

## 2. Maigret

* **GitHub:** [https://github.com/soxoj/maigret](https://github.com/soxoj/maigret)
* Similar to Sherlock but focused on higher accuracy and detailed reporting.

## 3. Osintgram

* **GitHub:** [https://github.com/Datalux/Osintgram](https://github.com/Datalux/Osintgram)
* Gathers publicly available information from Instagram profiles.

## 4. Blackbird

* **GitHub:** [https://github.com/p1ngul1n0/blackbird](https://github.com/p1ngul1n0/blackbird)
* Fast username search tool across many platforms.

## 5. Holehe

* **GitHub:** [https://github.com/megadose/holehe](https://github.com/megadose/holehe)
* Checks if an email address is registered on various websites.

## 6. GHunt

* **GitHub:** [https://github.com/mxrch/GHunt](https://github.com/mxrch/GHunt)
* Extracts publicly available information linked to Google accounts.

## 7. Have I Been Pwned

* **Website:** [https://haveibeenpwned.com](https://haveibeenpwned.com)
* Checks whether an email or phone number appears in known data breaches.

## 8. Shodan

* **Website:** [https://www.shodan.io](https://www.shodan.io)
* Search engine for internet-connected devices and exposed services.

## 9. Censys

* **Website:** [https://search.censys.io](https://search.censys.io)
* Internet-wide scanning and certificate analysis platform.

## 10. ExifTool

* **Website:** [https://exiftool.org](https://exiftool.org)
* Reads, writes, and edits metadata in files (images, PDFs, etc.).

## 11. SpiderFoot

* **Website:** [https://www.spiderfoot.net](https://www.spiderfoot.net)
* **GitHub:** [https://github.com/smicallef/spiderfoot](https://github.com/smicallef/spiderfoot)
* Automated OSINT collection and analysis platform.

## 12. Maltego

* **Website:** [https://www.maltego.com](https://www.maltego.com)
* Visual link analysis tool for mapping relationships between data.

## 13. Pastebin

* **Website:** [https://pastebin.com](https://pastebin.com)
* Text-sharing platform often used for data dumps and public disclosures.

## 14. Rentry

* **Website:** [https://rentry.co](https://rentry.co)
* Anonymous markdown-based publishing service.

## 15. PhoneInfoga

* **GitHub:** [https://github.com/sundowndev/phoneinfoga](https://github.com/sundowndev/phoneinfoga)
* OSINT framework for gathering information about phone numbers.

## 16. Creepy

* **GitHub:** [https://github.com/ilektrojohn/creepy](https://github.com/ilektrojohn/creepy)
* Geolocation OSINT tool based on social media data.

## 17. OSINT Framework

* **Website:** [https://osintframework.com](https://osintframework.com)
* Organized directory of OSINT tools and resources.

---

## 📌 How to Use This List

You can:

* Install tools locally (Python-based tools typically require `pip`)
* Use web-based platforms directly from your browser
* Integrate them into your cybersecurity lab or VM
